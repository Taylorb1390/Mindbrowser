Fun Fact.

Halloween all started as a pre-Christian Celtic festival called Samhain (which means "summer's end")
held around the first of November. It celebrated the final day of the harvest and the crossing of
spirits over into the other world. People in Ireland, the United Kingdom, and Northern France
would ward off ghosts by lighting sacrificial bonfires, and, you guessed it, wearing costumes, according to History.com.
